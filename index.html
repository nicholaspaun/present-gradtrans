This country doesn't actually exist!

What!?

  • Romanians traditionally lived in what is now Romania and Moldova (including the Transnistrian part).
  • Many other groups, including Russians also settled and controlled the area.
  • The Nistru river (the source of the name Transnistria) formed the traditional boundary between Romania and Ukraine/Russia.
  • During World War II, the Soviet Union annexed the Romanian territories of Bessarabia and Bucovina.
  • The new boundary between Romania and the Soviet Union became the Prut.

Transnistria is not recognized as an independent state

  • Most countries believe that this territory belongs to the Republic of Moldova.
  • Russia says that Transnistria has a special independent status, but that it is not a sovereign country.
  • Transnistria is recognized by Abkhazia, South Ossetia and the Nagorno-Karabakh Republic, none of which are internationally recognized.
    • Abkhazia and South Ossetia were taken by Russia in a similar wars against Georgia. The ethnic situation is more complicated there, however.
